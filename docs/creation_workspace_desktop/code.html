Chapter One

The Hearth of Silence

The valley had a way of swallowingly sound. It wasn't that the world was empty, but rather that the silence was of a different quality—a thick, textured quiet that felt like being submerged in cold water. Silas pulled his cloak tighter, the coarse wool rasping against his chin. He watched the last of the sun slip behind the jagged peaks, painting the slate roofs of the village in bruised purples and dying golds.

Inside the stone cottage, the fire was the only living thing. It snapped and crackled, throwing elongated shadows against the uneven walls that seemed to dance to a rhythm only they understood. Elias stood by the window, his silhouette sharp and unmoving. He had been looking out into the gathering gloom for nearly an hour, his fingers tracing the condensation on the glass.

"They're late," Elias said, his voice barely a murmur.

Silas didn't answer. He knew who 'they' were, and he knew why they were late. The pass was treacherous this time of year, and the rumors of the shadow-beasts were no longer just tavern tales told to frighten children.
